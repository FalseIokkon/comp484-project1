ITTF (International Table Tennis Federation)
The ITTF is the governing body for all international table tennis associations. It oversees the official rules of the sport, organizes professional competitions, and approves equipment standards such as ball size and table specifications. Learn more at the official ITTF website.
Rubber Information
Rubbers are incredibly unique and vary more than most would imagine. Rubbers come in two main categories: inverted and pimpled. Inverted rubbers than have their own sub-categories such as regular, tacky, hybrid, and more. Pimpled rubbers also have their own sub-categories and mainly fall under short, medium, and long pimpled rubbers. Each type of rubber completely changes the feel and response of the ball when hit, as well as provides opportunities for unique playstyles. The current most popular rubber amongst professional players is the DHS Hurricane rubber series.
Blade Information
Blades are the foundation of every table tennis racket and play just as important a role as the rubbers. They are typically made of multiple layers of wood, sometimes combined with carbon or other synthetic fibers to enhance speed and stability. Blades are often categorized by their playing style: defensive, all-round, and offensive. Defensive blades are slower and offer maximum control, while offensive blades are built for speed and power. The number of plies (layers) also affects performance, with 5-ply blades favoring control and 7-ply or composite blades offering more aggression. Choosing the right blade is highly personal, as its weight, handle shape, and flexibility all influence how a player feels and performs on the table.
